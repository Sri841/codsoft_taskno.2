✦ 5 Iconic Dishes

Punjab

The land of five rivers and unmatched hospitality. Punjabi food is bold, rich, and unapologetically generous — a cuisine where butter isn't a condiment, it's a philosophy.

🏆 Iconic #1

Butter Chicken

Tender tandoor-kissed chicken dunked in a velvety tomato-butter-cream gravy. Born in Delhi, claimed by every Punjabi household — and the world's most ordered Indian dish.

🍳 How to Make Marinate chicken in yoghurt & spices, grill in tandoor, then simmer in rich tomato-butter-cream sauce with fenugreek (kasuri methi) for the signature smoky-sweet flavour.
🌿 Seasonal #2

Sarson da Saag

A winter-harvest delicacy of slow-cooked mustard greens, spinach, and bathua, finished with a generous dollop of desi ghee and ginger. Winter evenings made edible.

🍳 How to Make Boil mustard greens with spinach, blend partially, sauté with onion, garlic, ginger. Finish with white butter. Best served with Makki di Roti.
🌽 Staple #3

Makki di Roti

Thick, unleavened corn-flour flatbread cooked on a tawa till golden. The essential companion to Sarson da Saag — rustic, wholesome, and deeply satisfying.

🍳 How to Make Knead yellow maize flour with warm water into a soft dough. Pat by hand onto hot tawa, cook on both sides till golden spots appear. Serve hot with white butter.
🥘 Street Legend #4

Chole Bhature

Spiced chickpea curry so bold it'll wake you up, paired with a cloud-light deep-fried bhatura. Punjab's definitive breakfast — eaten morning till midnight.

🍳 How to Make Cook soaked chickpeas with tea bags (for colour), sauté with whole spices & tomatoes. Make bhatura dough with maida, yoghurt, baking soda; deep-fry till puffed golden.
🥛 Drink #5

Lassi

Cool, creamy, churned yoghurt drink served sweet with rose water and cream, or salted with cumin. The Punjabi answer to every question — especially summer.

🍳 How to Make Whip full-fat yoghurt in a blender with cold water, sugar (or salt), and cardamom. Top with a thick layer of malai (clotted cream) and a pinch of saffron.

✦ 5 Iconic Dishes

West Bengal

Where rivers meet artistry — Bengali cuisine balances the pungency of mustard oil, the sweetness of hilsa, and the joy of mishti doi with extraordinary refinement.

🐟 Beloved #1

Macher Jhol

A light, golden fish curry with potatoes, turmeric, and cumin — cooked in mustard oil. Not spicy but deeply flavoured, the heartbeat of a Bengali household.

🍳 How to Make Fry marinated fish in mustard oil. Sauté potatoes with panch phoron, turmeric, green chillies. Add water, simmer with fish till gravy is thin and fragrant.
🥩 Slow Cooked #2

Kosha Mangsho

Dark, slow-braised mutton curry with caramelised onions and deep whole spices. The Sunday ritual of every Bengali home — worth every hour of patient cooking.

🍳 How to Make Marinate mutton in yoghurt, spices, raw papaya. Slow-cook with caramelised onion paste on low flame till oil separates. Serve with luchi or rice.
👑 Signature #3

Shorshe Ilish

Hilsa fish — the queen of Bengali rivers — steamed or fried in a potent mustard-green chilli paste. A taste so singular it's nearly sacred to Bengalis.

🍳 How to Make Grind mustard seeds with green chillies and salt into a paste. Coat hilsa pieces, add mustard oil and turmeric. Steam or bake in a banana-leaf parcel for 15 min.
🌯 Street Food #4

Kathi Roll

Flaky egg-coated paratha wrapped around spiced chicken or mutton, raw onion, green chutney, and lime. Born on Kolkata's Park Street, now conquered every city.

🍳 How to Make Dip paratha in beaten egg, fry till crisp. Fill with grilled chicken tikka, sliced onion, green chutney, and a squeeze of lime. Roll tight and serve in paper.
🍮 Dessert #5

Mishti Doi

Sweet, fermented yoghurt set in clay pots overnight — caramel-hued, silky, and gentle on the palate. The quiet, perfect ending to every Bengali meal.

🍳 How to Make Reduce full-fat milk with caramelised sugar till slightly thick. Cool to lukewarm, mix in yoghurt culture, pour into earthen pots. Set overnight in a warm place.

✦ 5 Iconic Dishes

Tamil Nadu

A cuisine rooted in ancient Sangam traditions — where rice, lentils, and tamarind form an eternal trinity, and every meal on a banana leaf is a meditation.

⭐ World Famous #1

Dosa

Crisp, lace-thin crepe of fermented rice-lentil batter, served with coconut chutney and sambar. Masala dosa variant stuffed with spiced potato mash is a global icon.

🍳 How to Make Soak & grind rice and urad dal separately, combine, ferment 8–12 hours. Pour thin circles on hot tawa, drizzle oil, cook till crisp and golden. Serve with chutneys.
🌅 Breakfast #2

Idli

Soft, pillowy steam-cooked rice cakes — featherlight, gut-friendly, and deeply satisfying. Tamil Nadu's gift to the world's breakfast tables. Dunk in sambar; bliss.

🍳 How to Make Ferment rice and urad dal batter overnight. Pour into greased idli moulds, steam for 10–12 minutes. Serve hot with coconut chutney and tomato-based sambar.
🥘 Staple #3

Sambar

Tangy tamarind and toor dal stew loaded with drumstick, tomatoes, and pearl onions — spiced with sambar powder. The glue that holds South Indian cuisine together.

🍳 How to Make Cook toor dal, add tamarind extract, vegetables, and sambar masala. Finish with a mustard-curry leaf-red chilli tempering in sesame oil. Simmer 5 min. Done.
🌾 Harvest #4

Pongal

Harvest-festival rice-and-lentil porridge tempered with black pepper, cashews, and ghee — ven pongal (savoury) for mornings, sweet sakkarai pongal for celebrations.

🍳 How to Make Pressure-cook rice with moong dal till mushy. Temper ghee with mustard, cumin, black pepper, curry leaves, cashews. Mix in, add salt. Serve hot with chutney.
🌶️ Fiery #5

Chettinad Chicken

The jewel of Chettinad cuisine — chicken cooked in an explosive spice paste of kalpasi, marathi mokku, and stone-flower. Complex, aromatic, and thrillingly bold.

🍳 How to Make Grind fresh Chettinad spice masala with coconut. Sauté with onions, tomatoes, and chicken. Cook covered on slow flame till oil separates and meat is tender.

✦ 5 Iconic Dishes

Hyderabad · Telangana

A city where Mughal opulence meets Deccan earthy-ness. Hyderabadi food is an event, not just a meal — layered, perfumed, and royally proportioned.

👑 Royal #1

Hyderabadi Biryani

Slow-cooked dum biryani with fragrant basmati, caramelised onions, saffron, and marinated meat sealed inside a pot — the Nizams' greatest gift to humanity.

🍳 How to Make Layer half-cooked rice over marinated meat in a pot. Seal with dough, cook on dum (steam) for 45 min. Open at the table for maximum drama and aroma.
🥘 Slow Cooked #2

Haleem

A thick, porridge-like stew of wheat, barley, and slow-cooked mutton — stirred for hours until silky. A Ramzan staple now eaten year-round with lime and crispy onions.

🍳 How to Make Soak broken wheat overnight. Cook mutton till falling-apart, combine with wheat, spice, and stock. Stir continuously 2–3 hours till paste-like. Garnish liberally.
🔥 Grilled #3

Kebabs

Seekh kebabs, shami kebabs, and pathar ka gosht — smoked over charcoal to juicy perfection. Hyderabad's streets smell of these from dusk to midnight.

🍳 How to Make Mince mutton with spices, onion, and green chillies. Shape onto skewers (seekh) or patties (shami). Grill on charcoal or tawa till caramelised. Serve with mint chutney.
🍮 Dessert #4

Double Ka Meetha

Fried bread (double roti) soaked in rich reduced milk and sugar, garnished with saffron, cardamom, and dry fruits. Hyderabad's answer to bread pudding — infinitely better.

🍳 How to Make Deep-fry bread slices till golden. Soak in warm sugar syrup, then layer with thickened rabdi milk. Garnish with pistachios, saffron, and rose water. Serve warm.
🌶️ Fiery #5

Mirchi Ka Salan

Big green chillies in a nutty, tangy gravy of peanuts, sesame, and tamarind — the traditional accompaniment to Hyderabadi biryani. Spicy, creamy, and addictive.

🍳 How to Make Fry whole green chillies. Blend peanuts, sesame, coconut, and dry-roasted spices. Sauté masala, add tamarind water and chillies. Simmer till thick and glossy.

✦ 5 Iconic Dishes

Gujarat

Predominantly vegetarian yet endlessly inventive — Gujarati cuisine balances sweet, sour, and spicy in every bite, proving that vegetables need no apology.

⭐ Staple #1

Dhokla

Soft, spongy steamed cakes of fermented chickpea flour — tangy, subtly sweet, and light enough to eat endlessly. A snack that doubles as a meal of conscience.

🍳 How to Make Ferment besan (gram flour) batter with curd, add Eno, steam 15 min. Temper with mustard seeds, green chillies, and curry leaves. Drizzle lemon juice and garnish.
🌿 Everyday #2

Thepla

Spiced flatbread of whole wheat and fenugreek leaves — soft yet sturdy, made for travel. The Gujarati traveller's best companion on every train journey ever taken.

🍳 How to Make Knead whole wheat flour with fresh methi, besan, yoghurt, turmeric, and chilli powder. Roll thin, cook on tawa with oil till brown spots appear. Store up to 3 days.
🎨 Artisanal #3

Khandvi

Delicate rolls of cooked chickpea-yoghurt batter spread paper-thin and rolled. One of the most technically demanding snacks in all Indian cooking — and worth every effort.

🍳 How to Make Cook besan-curd-water mixture while stirring till thick. Spread thin on a greased surface. Cool, roll into spirals. Temper with mustard, sesame, and green chilli.
❄️ Winter Special #4

Undhiyu

A glorious winter casserole of seasonal vegetables, raw banana, purple yam, and muthia — traditionally slow-cooked underground upside-down in a clay pot.

🍳 How to Make Layer mixed winter vegetables with methi muthia, and a masala of coconut, coriander, and sesame. Cook slow in a sealed vessel or pressure cooker till tender and melded.
🥨 Crunchy #5

Fafda

Crispy, coiled gram-flour strips fried to a snappy golden — the quintessential Gujarati Sunday breakfast with papaya chutney and jalebi. Crunch therapy.

🍳 How to Make Knead besan with ajwain, turmeric, and oil into a stiff dough. Roll or extrude into strips, deep-fry in medium oil till golden and crisp. Serve with raw papaya chutney.

✦ 5 Iconic Dishes

Rajasthan

Born of desert survival and royal excess — Rajasthani food is bold, preserved, and spectacularly spiced. A cuisine where scarcity became artistry.

🏆 Legend #1

Dal Baati Churma

The royal triad — hard wheat baati baked over cow-dung cakes, dunked in five-lentil dal, with sweet crumbled churma. A meal so complete it's practically a festival.

🍳 How to Make Knead wheat-ghee dough, shape into balls, bake in oven or over fire. Cook panchmel dal with Rajasthani spices. Break baati, drown in dal and ghee. Serve churma aside.
🌿 Vegetarian #2

Gatte ki Sabzi

Gram flour dumplings in a tangy, fiery yoghurt-based gravy with whole spices. Rajasthan's ingenious solution to the desert's lack of fresh vegetables — addictively good.

🍳 How to Make Boil spiced besan-dough cylinders, slice into pieces. Fry briefly. Simmer in a temperedcurd gravy. Cook till gravy thickens and gatte absorb the flavour. Serve with baati.
🏜️ Desert Gem #3

Ker Sangri

Dried desert berries (ker) and dried sangri beans cooked in mustard oil with whole spices and dry red chillies. The ultimate desert pantry dish — earthy and uncompromising.

🍳 How to Make Soak ker and sangri overnight. Drain, fry in mustard oil with whole spices and dried chillies. Add salt and amchur. Cook dry. Stores for days — a true desert staple.
🔥 Bold #4

Laal Maas

Fiery, blood-red mutton curry made with Mathania chillies and game-hunting traditions. The fearless fire of Rajasthan in a bowl — not for the faint-hearted.

🍳 How to Make Marinate mutton in red Mathania chilli paste. Sauté with ghee, whole spices, and onions. Add yoghurt, cook on slow flame 90 min till oil rises and mutton is fall-apart tender.
🍮 Festive #5

Ghewar

A disc-shaped honeycomb sweet of maida batter fried in ghee and drenched in sugar syrup — topped with rabdi and saffron. The star of Teej and Raksha Bandhan.

🍳 How to Make Drizzle thin maida batter into a mould of hot ghee, building a latticed disc layer by layer. Soak in hot sugar syrup. Top with thickened rabdi, pistachios, and silver varq.
